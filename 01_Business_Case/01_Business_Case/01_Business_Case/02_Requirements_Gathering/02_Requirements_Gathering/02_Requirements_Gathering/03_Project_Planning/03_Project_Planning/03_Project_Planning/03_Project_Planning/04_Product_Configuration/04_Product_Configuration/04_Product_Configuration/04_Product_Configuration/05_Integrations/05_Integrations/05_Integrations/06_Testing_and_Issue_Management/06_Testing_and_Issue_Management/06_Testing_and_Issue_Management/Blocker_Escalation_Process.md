# Blocker Escalation Process 🚦

## Purpose

This document defines how blockers and critical issues should be escalated during the implementation of ABC GmbH’s corporate travel booking SaaS platform.

The goal is to prevent delays, create clear ownership, and ensure that risks are communicated early with practical next steps.

## What Is a Blocker?

A blocker is an issue that prevents progress on a key implementation activity.

Examples include:

* SSO login testing cannot begin
* Employee data file is missing or incomplete
* Approval workflow rules are not confirmed
* Critical configuration issue is unresolved
* Finance reporting requirements are unclear
* Client sign-off is delayed before go-live

## Escalation Principles

* Raise blockers early, not at the last minute
* Always include the business impact
* Assign a clear owner
* Provide recommended next steps
* Track the blocker until resolved
* Communicate updates during status meetings

## Escalation Levels

| Level   | Situation                                            | Action                                                  | Owner                                    |
| ------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Level 1 | Minor issue with low project impact                  | Track in issue log and follow up with owner             | Implementation Manager                   |
| Level 2 | Issue may delay a task or milestone                  | Raise in weekly status meeting with next steps          | Implementation Manager                   |
| Level 3 | Issue may delay go-live or affect customer readiness | Escalate to Project Sponsor and relevant team lead      | Implementation Manager                   |
| Level 4 | Critical blocker prevents launch                     | Escalate immediately with options and decision required | Project Sponsor / Implementation Manager |

## Blocker Escalation Template

When escalating a blocker, include:

* Blocker description
* Impact on timeline or go-live
* Current owner
* Decision needed
* Recommended solution
* Deadline for response
* Alternative options

## Example Escalation

### Blocker

SSO testing has not started because the client IT team has not confirmed the identity provider details.

### Impact

Login testing may be delayed, which could affect go-live readiness.

### Owner

Client IT Contact

### Recommended Next Step

Schedule a technical review call within 48 hours and define a fallback login process if SSO cannot be completed before go-live.

### Escalation Level

Level 3

## Communication Cadence

| Meeting / Update                     | Frequency           | Purpose                                              |
| ------------------------------------ | ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Weekly implementation status meeting | Weekly              | Review risks, issues, milestones, and open decisions |
| Client update email                  | Weekly or as needed | Summarize progress, blockers, and decisions needed   |
| Internal technical follow-up         | As needed           | Resolve configuration or integration blockers        |
| Go-live readiness review             | Final week          | Confirm launch readiness and open risks              |

## Implementation Manager Responsibilities

The Implementation Manager is responsible for:

* Identifying blockers early
* Documenting blockers clearly
* Assigning owners and next steps
* Communicating impact to stakeholders
* Escalating when timelines or go-live readiness are at risk
* Tracking resolution until closure

## Business Value

A clear blocker escalation process helps reduce project delays, improve stakeholder accountability, and protect go-live readiness.
